Klavi — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026

Klavi is designed to be private by default. It runs entirely on your Mac.

What we collect

Nothing. Klavi does not collect, store, log, or transmit any personal data or anything you type. There are no analytics, no advertising SDKs, no crash reporters, and no network connections of any kind.

Accessibility access

Klavi requires macOS Accessibility permission. It is used solely to:

  • detect the keyboard layout a word was typed in, and
  • switch the layout and re-type the word correctly.

Keystrokes are processed in memory, on your device, in real time, and are never written to disk or sent anywhere. Klavi does not read secure input (password) fields.

Data sharing

Klavi shares no data with the developer or any third party, because it collects none.

Changes

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be published on this page.
